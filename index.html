People, Places, and Things


People, Places and Things by Duncan Macmillan follows Emma, an actress struggling with addiction, as she enters rehab after a public breakdown. Resistant to the recovery process, she challenges the program’s principles while grappling with her identity and the truth about herself. The play explores the chaos of addiction, the difficulty of confronting one’s inner demons, and the nonlinear path of recovery. Through vivid and disorienting staging, the audience experiences Emma’s distorted reality as she fights to reclaim control over her life.






Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf?


Who’s Afraid of Virginia Woolf? by Edward Albee is a dark and intense drama that follows George and Martha, a middle-aged married couple, as they invite a younger couple, Nick and Honey, to their home after a college faculty party. Over the course of a long, alcohol-fueled night, the couples engage in a series of brutal, emotionally charged games that expose lies, secrets, and deep-seated frustrations. George and Martha’s volatile relationship takes center stage as their arguments escalate, revealing a tragic, invented narrative about their “son.” The younger couple becomes entangled in the chaos, with Nick and Honey’s own vulnerabilities and marital issues coming to light. By the end of the night, the truth is laid bare, leaving all four characters emotionally shattered and forever changed. The play is a raw exploration of truth, illusion, love, and the destructive power of repressed pain.






The Good Place


The Good Place by Michael Schur is a smart and heartwarming comedy that follows Eleanor Shellstrop, who wakes up in a utopia designed for people who led exceptionally good lives on Earth. However, Eleanor quickly realizes there’s been a mistake—she doesn’t belong there, as her life was far from virtuous. To avoid being found out, she turns to Chidi, a kind-hearted ethics professor, to teach her how to be a better person. Alongside new friends Tahani, a glamorous socialite, and Jason, a well-meaning but clueless DJ, Eleanor works to fit in while grappling with the challenges of the afterlife. Together, they discover unexpected truths about themselves, the meaning of goodness, and what it takes to truly live an ethical life.
